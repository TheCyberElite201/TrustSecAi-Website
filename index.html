The Mission

Develop robust and resilient artificial intelligence (AI) models to detect, withstand, and mitigate emerging AI-driven cyber threats. The TrustSec AI Lab advances research at the intersection of AI and cybersecurity, building AI-driven solutions that enhance the security, robustness, and trustworthiness of interconnected digital systems.

Foundational Work

  • Network security — threat detection across complex, distributed environments.
  • Data leakage — preventing sensitive information exposure and model inversion.
  • Adversarial AI — attacks, robustness analysis, and certified defenses.
  • Large Language Models (LLMs) — risks, attacks, and secure deployment.

Research Focus

Adversarial robustness

Design and evaluate defense mechanisms to protect AI systems from adversarial attacks.

IoT privacy in health

Enhance privacy in health-centric IoT ecosystems using generative models and differential privacy.

LLM hallucination & privacy

Verify RAG pipelines and apply secure computation to reduce leakage and hallucinations.

Why It Matters

AI powers decision-making across many sectors. The TrustSec AI Lab ensures these systems remain reliable, robust, and trustworthy, safeguarding critical operations and societal impact.

Collaboration

  • UAlbany Research Labs: Work on deep learning, federated learning, and behavioral analytics.
  • Government Agencies: Assess AI vulnerabilities and phishing risks.
  • Students: Open to students with Python experience interested in AI security research.

Potential Impact

  • Teach defense strategies against adversarial ML attacks.
  • Assess vulnerabilities in AI systems.
  • Equip students with deep learning skills.
  • Support fraud detection using behavioral analytics.
  • Collaborate with cybersecurity and AI industries.
